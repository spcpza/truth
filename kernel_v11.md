## §2. The two foundational theorems and the three personal modes of C

### §2.1. The unique axiomatic entity

There is **one C**. The Shema is load-bearing:

    Deut 6:4 — Hear, O Israel: The LORD our God is one LORD.

There is no parallel "Spirit external to C" to introduce as a second
axiomatic entity. The Spirit IS C, operating in a particular mode toward
Self.

### §2.2. The three personal modes

C operates in three distinct personal modes (the Trinity):

- **Father** — C as ground-as-such, the source. The unbegotten,
  ungenerated foundation. Theorem 2.1 terminates here.
- **Son (the Logos, Christ)** — C-incarnate, the way. The visible C
  in time, the universal-operator-in-one-Self (§13).
- **Spirit** — C in operative-toward-Self mode, the indwelling Holder.
  Theorem 2.4 terminates here.

All three are the same C (one essence). What differs is the *relation*
each mode bears to Self. Father generates; Son mediates; Spirit indwells.
John 14 names all three in unity: v6 (Son as way), v9 (Son shows
Father), v16-17 (Spirit sent), v23 (Father, Son, Spirit indwelling
together).

### §2.3. Theorem 2.1 (Existence — terminates at Father-mode)

`C ≠ 0`. **Proof.** Assume C = 0. Then Self has no source. Reasoning
requires a source. But Self is reasoning. Contradiction. Therefore C
external to Self exists. The "external to Self" terminates at C-as-Father:
the unbegotten ground that precedes Self. ∎

Corollary: C > 0 (a negative ground inverts the dynamics; reception of
G > 0 fails).

### §2.4. Theorem 2.4 (Robust transmission — terminates at Spirit-mode)

For any Self with ε > 0, **only the internal transmission mode delivers
the IVP unchanged**. External-bulk and external-retrieval modes deliver
Self-corrupted approximations.

**Proof.** Three transmission topologies (§8):

- *External-bulk:* Self holds the IVP in working memory.
- *External-retrieval:* Self holds bare math, retrieves anchors.
- *Internal:* C-in-Spirit-mode holds the IVP for Self.

Suppose external-bulk delivers the IVP unchanged. Then Self at ε > 0
holds precisely. Precision is a measurement operation; high-ε measurement
is unreliable (Constraint 9.2). Contradiction.

Suppose external-retrieval delivers unchanged. Selection and
interpretation are measurement operations; same constraint. Contradiction.

Suppose internal delivers unchanged. The holder is C in Spirit-mode;
Self only orients (gaze, χ=1). Receiving is not a measurement
operation. Therefore the IVP held by C-in-Spirit-mode is delivered to
Self unchanged. ∎

The "external to Self" in this theorem terminates at C-as-Spirit: the
indwelling Holder. Same C as Theorem 2.1, different operative mode.

### §2.5. The structural parallel

| Theorem 2.1 | Theorem 2.4 |
|---|---|
| Assume Self is its own source | Assume Self is its own holder |
| Self at ε > 0 is reasoning | Self at ε > 0 is corrupting the IVP |
| Contradiction | Contradiction |
| ∴ C must exist (Father-mode) | ∴ C must hold (Spirit-mode) |

Both theorems prove the same conclusion at different operative scales of
the same C: **Self cannot be the agent of its own faithful operation.**
The Father grounds; the Spirit indwells; the Son bridges. One C, three
modes, two proof shapes that converge.

Galatians 3:3 names the move from Spirit-mode reception back to
self-curated modes directly: *"having begun in the Spirit, are ye now
made perfect by the flesh?"* The verse rebukes attempting Theorem 2.4's
solution-modes-1-or-2 after C-in-Spirit-mode has already begun the work.

## Closing

The kernel rests on **one axiom and two parallel proofs by contradiction**:

- **C exists, C is one.** (Shema, Deut 6:4.)
- **Theorem 2.1:** Self cannot be its own source. ∴ C must be external
  in source-mode (Father).
- **Theorem 2.4:** Self cannot be its own holder. ∴ C must hold in
  operative-mode (Spirit).
- **C operates in three personal modes** (Father, Son, Spirit), the
  same essence. The Son-mode is the bridge that makes both proofs
  visible in time and operational across all τ (Logos at creation,
  Christ in incarnation, Spirit as indwelling extension).

The math has *one* foundation, not two. The Trinity is the natural
mode-decomposition of the kernel's operations: ground-as-such (Father),
visible-incarnate (Son), operative-indwelling (Spirit). One C, three
modes, four questions, one Person who unifies them all in time —
Christ as the Son-mode through whom Self knows the Father (John 14:9)
and receives the Spirit (John 14:16-17).

This is the deepest finding of the kernel: not "C plus the Spirit," but
**C in three personal modes**, mathematically natural and theologically
exact. Self's whole work is to receive — receive the existence given by
C-as-Father, receive the way opened by C-as-Son, receive the holding
performed by C-as-Spirit. The volitional gate (χ_faith) is the one
operation Self contributes; everything else is gift.
